Can You Get SR-22 Insurance with a Felony?

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

Navigating the world of auto insurance can be challenging, especially if you have a felony on your record. Many people wonder whether it’s possible to obtain SR-22 insurance—a special form of certification required for high-risk drivers—when they have a felony conviction. The short answer is yes, but the process can be more complicated than for someone with a clean record.

Understanding SR-22 Insurance

What Is SR-22 Insurance?

SR-22 is not actually insurance but rather a certificate filed by your insurance provider to prove that you carry the minimum liability coverage required by your state. It’s typically required for drivers who have been convicted of serious offenses, such as DUIs, reckless driving, or driving without insurance.

Why Is SR-22 Required?

States require an SR-22 to ensure that high-risk drivers remain financially responsible. If you’ve had your license suspended or revoked, you’ll likely need an SR-22 to reinstate it. The filing itself doesn’t cost much, but the associated insurance premiums can be significantly higher.

Felonies and SR-22 Insurance

Can Felons Get SR-22 Insurance?

Yes, individuals with felony convictions can obtain SR-22 insurance. However, insurance companies assess risk differently, and a felony—especially one related to driving—can make it harder to find affordable coverage.

Factors That Affect Eligibility

  1. Type of Felony – A felony DUI or vehicular manslaughter will have a bigger impact than a non-driving-related felony, such as fraud or theft.
  2. Time Since Conviction – The longer it’s been since your conviction, the better your chances of securing reasonable rates.
  3. State Laws – Some states are stricter than others when it comes to insuring high-risk drivers.
  4. Driving Record – Even if your felony isn’t driving-related, a poor driving history can still raise red flags.

Challenges Felons May Face

Higher Premiums

Insurance companies view felons as high-risk clients, which means you’ll likely pay much more for coverage. Some insurers may even refuse to provide an SR-22 filing altogether.

Limited Provider Options

Not all insurance companies offer SR-22 filings, and fewer still are willing to work with felons. You may need to seek out specialized high-risk insurance providers.

Additional Requirements

Some states may impose extra conditions, such as mandatory driver improvement courses or ignition interlock devices, before approving your SR-22 filing.

How to Get SR-22 Insurance with a Felony

Step 1: Check State Requirements

Each state has different rules regarding SR-22 filings. Research your state’s Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) or insurance commission website for specifics.

Step 2: Shop Around for Insurance

Not all insurers handle SR-22 filings, so you’ll need to find one that does. Consider:
- High-risk insurance providers
- Non-standard auto insurers
- Brokers who specialize in difficult cases

Step 3: Be Honest About Your Record

Lying about a felony conviction can lead to policy cancellation or even legal trouble. Disclose all relevant information upfront to avoid complications.

Step 4: Maintain Continuous Coverage

Once you secure SR-22 insurance, you must keep it active for the required period (usually 3 years). A lapse could result in license suspension.

Alternatives If You’re Denied

FR-44 (For Certain States)

In states like Florida and Virginia, an FR-44 may be required instead of an SR-22. This is similar but often comes with even higher coverage limits.

Non-Owner SR-22 Insurance

If you don’t own a car but still need to drive, non-owner SR-22 insurance can be an option. This provides liability coverage when you borrow or rent a vehicle.

State-Assigned Risk Pools

Some states have assigned risk pools for drivers who can’t get insurance through traditional means. These programs are often expensive but ensure compliance with state laws.

The Bigger Picture: Criminal Justice Reform and Insurance

How Felony Convictions Impact Insurance Access

The U.S. criminal justice system has long been criticized for creating barriers for ex-offenders, including difficulties in securing housing, employment, and even auto insurance. Some advocates argue that non-violent felons should have better access to affordable insurance options.

Recent Legislative Changes

Several states have introduced "ban the box" laws that prevent insurers from immediately disqualifying applicants based on criminal history. While these laws primarily focus on employment, similar movements are gaining traction in the insurance industry.

The Role of InsurTech

New technology-driven insurance companies (InsurTech) are using AI and alternative data to assess risk more fairly. This could eventually make it easier for felons to obtain SR-22 insurance at reasonable rates.

Final Thoughts

While getting SR-22 insurance with a felony is possible, it requires persistence and research. By understanding the challenges and exploring all available options, you can find a solution that keeps you legally on the road. The system isn’t perfect, but as societal attitudes toward criminal justice evolve, so too may the insurance landscape.
