Jackson National Life Insurance: How to Handle Policy Surrenders

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

Life insurance is a critical component of financial planning, offering security and peace of mind. However, circumstances change, and policyholders may find themselves considering surrendering their Jackson National Life Insurance policy. Whether due to financial strain, shifting priorities, or better alternatives, understanding how to handle a policy surrender is essential. This guide explores the process, implications, and alternatives to surrendering a life insurance policy in today’s economic climate.

Understanding Policy Surrenders

A policy surrender occurs when a policyholder voluntarily terminates their life insurance contract before maturity or death benefit payout. In exchange, the insurance company provides a cash surrender value (CSV), which is the accumulated savings portion minus any fees or penalties.

Why Policyholders Surrender Their Policies

Several factors drive policy surrenders, especially in today’s volatile economy:

  1. Financial Hardship – Rising inflation, job losses, or unexpected expenses may force individuals to liquidate assets, including life insurance.
  2. Changing Needs – A policy purchased years ago may no longer align with current financial goals (e.g., children are grown, mortgage is paid off).
  3. Better Investment Opportunities – Some may prefer redirecting funds into higher-yield investments like stocks or real estate.
  4. Dissatisfaction with Policy Performance – If the policy’s returns underperform expectations, surrendering may seem like the best option.

The Surrender Process with Jackson National Life Insurance

Before proceeding with a surrender, policyholders should follow these steps:

1. Review the Policy Terms

Jackson National’s policies vary in surrender charges, grace periods, and CSV calculations. Check the contract for:
- Surrender Charge Schedule – Fees typically decrease over time.
- Accrued Cash Value – Confirm how much will be received after deductions.
- Tax Implications – Surrendering may trigger taxable income if CSV exceeds premiums paid.

2. Explore Alternatives to Surrendering

Surrendering isn’t always the best move. Consider:
- Policy Loans – Borrow against the CSV without terminating coverage.
- Reduced Paid-Up Insurance – Lower the death benefit but keep the policy active.
- 1035 Exchange – Tax-free transfer to another insurance or annuity product.

3. Contact Jackson National’s Customer Service

Initiate the surrender by:
- Submitting a written request.
- Providing policy details and identification.
- Specifying payout method (check, direct deposit).

4. Await Processing and Payout

Surrenders typically take 7–14 days. The payout will reflect the CSV minus any outstanding loans or fees.

Financial and Tax Consequences

Surrendering a policy isn’t free of repercussions:

Taxable Gains

If the CSV exceeds total premiums paid, the difference is taxed as ordinary income. For example:
- Premiums paid: $50,000
- CSV received: $65,000
- Taxable gain: $15,000

Loss of Coverage

Terminating the policy leaves beneficiaries unprotected. In a post-pandemic world, where health uncertainties persist, this risk is significant.

Surrender Charges

Early surrenders often incur hefty fees, reducing the payout.

Global Economic Factors Influencing Surrenders

Today’s macroeconomic trends play a role in policyholder decisions:

Inflation and Rising Interest Rates

High inflation erodes purchasing power, making policy premiums harder to afford. Meanwhile, rising interest rates make alternative investments (e.g., bonds) more attractive.

Market Volatility

Stock market fluctuations may push individuals to liquidate insurance policies for liquidity.

Regulatory Changes

Shifts in tax laws or insurance regulations could impact surrender strategies.

Case Study: Surrendering vs. Keeping a Policy

Consider John, a 45-year-old with a $500,000 Jackson National universal life policy:
- Surrender Option – He receives $30,000 CSV but loses coverage and pays taxes on $5,000 gains.
- Alternative – He takes a $25,000 loan, keeps the policy, and repays over time.

In a high-inflation environment, retaining coverage while accessing cash may be wiser.

Final Thoughts

Surrendering a life insurance policy is a major financial decision. Policyholders must weigh immediate cash needs against long-term security. Jackson National Life Insurance offers flexibility, but exploring alternatives could preserve both liquidity and protection.

By staying informed and consulting a financial advisor, individuals can navigate surrenders strategically in today’s unpredictable economy.
