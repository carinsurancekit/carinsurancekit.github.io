Farmers Insurance Claims: How to File for Roof Claims

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

The sky has been different lately. Whether it’s the ferocious hurricanes battering the coastlines, the unprecedented hail storms pummeling the Midwest, or the relentless atmospheric rivers saturating the West, our roofs are on the front lines of a changing climate. Your roof is more than just shingles and plywood; it's your family's primary shield against the elements. When that shield is compromised, the stress can be overwhelming. Where do you start? Who can you trust? How do you ensure you receive a fair settlement to restore your home's integrity?

Navigating the insurance claims process, especially for something as significant as a roof, can feel like deciphering a complex code during a storm. But you don’t have to face it alone. This comprehensive guide is designed to walk you through every step of filing a roof claim with Farmers Insurance, empowering you with the knowledge and confidence to secure the recovery your home deserves.

Before the Storm: Proactive Steps for Every Homeowner

The most successful insurance claims begin long before the damage occurs. Being prepared is your greatest advantage.

Know Your Policy Inside and Out

Your Farmers Insurance policy is a contract, and understanding its terms is non-negotiable. Don't wait for an emergency to find out what's covered. Focus on these key sections:

  • Coverage A – Dwelling: This is the part of your policy that typically covers the structure of your home, including the roof. Check your policy's declarations page for your specific coverage limit.
  • Deductible: This is the amount you are responsible for paying out-of-pocket before your insurance coverage kicks in. Note whether you have a standard deductible or a higher percentage-based deductible for specific perils like hurricanes or windstorms.
  • Covered Perils: Most standard homeowners policies (HO-3) are "all-risk" on the dwelling, meaning they cover all causes of loss except those specifically excluded. Common exclusions can include wear and tear, neglect, and sometimes mold. It’s critical to know what isn’t covered.
  • Replacement Cost Value (RCV) vs. Actual Cash Value (ACV): This is a crucial distinction.
    • RCV: This means Farmers will pay to replace your damaged roof with a new one of similar kind and quality, without deducting for depreciation. This is the more comprehensive coverage.
    • ACV: This settlement is calculated as RCV minus depreciation. For an older roof, an ACV payout might be significantly lower and may not cover the full cost of a replacement.

Document Everything: Create a "Healthy Home" File

Take proactive steps to document the condition of your roof and property.

  • Perform Seasonal Visual Inspections: From the ground with binoculars, look for loose, cracked, or missing shingles; damaged flashing around chimneys and vents; and excessive granules in your gutters (a sign of aging shingles).
  • Keep a Maintenance Log: Record any repairs, cleanings, or inspections you have done. This proves you have maintained your roof properly.
  • Take "Before" Photos and Videos: When your roof is in good condition, take clear, dated photos and videos from all angles. This provides undeniable proof of your roof's pre-damage state.

The Storm Has Hit: Immediate Post-Damage Actions

Safety is the absolute priority. Once the immediate danger has passed, follow these steps.

Ensure Safety First

Do not risk your safety. If you suspect serious structural damage, evacuate the area. If there is a leak, place buckets to collect water and move valuables out of the way. Avoid walking on a damaged roof, as it can be unstable and dangerous.

Perform a Preliminary Assessment and Document the Damage

From a safe vantage point on the ground, document the damage thoroughly.

  • Photograph and Video Everything: Capture wide shots of the entire roof and close-ups of all damaged areas. Include photos of hail dents on gutters and downspouts, damaged siding, and any interior water damage.
  • Make Temporary Repairs: To prevent further damage, you can make temporary, reasonable repairs, such as placing a tarp over a leaky section of the roof. Important: Keep all receipts for materials and labor, as these costs are typically reimbursable under your policy.

The Claims Process: A Step-by-Step Guide with Farmers Insurance

Now, you're ready to initiate the formal process.

Step 1: Notify Farmers Insurance Promptly

Contact Farmers as soon as possible to start your claim. You can do this through their mobile app, website, or by calling their 24/7 claims hotline. Be prepared to provide: * Your policy number. * The date and estimated time the damage occurred. * A brief, general description of the damage. * Your contact information.

Step 2: The Farmers Insurance Adjuster Inspection

Farmers will assign a claims adjuster to your case. The adjuster's role is to inspect the damage, assess the cause, and write an estimate for the repair costs.

  • Be Present for the Inspection: This is highly recommended. You can point out all the damage you've observed and provide your documentation.
  • Hire a Professional Roofer for a Separate Estimate: Before the adjuster's inspection, consider having a reputable, local roofing contractor perform their own inspection and provide a detailed, line-item estimate. This gives you an independent assessment to compare against the adjuster's estimate. A good roofer can often identify subtle damage that an adjuster might miss.

Step 3: Understanding the Claim Settlement and Supplements

After the inspection, the Farmers adjuster will produce a claim summary and an estimate. Review this document carefully with your roofing contractor.

  • The Initial Settlement: Farmers will often issue an initial payment. This is frequently the Actual Cash Value (ACV) of the roof, which is the Replacement Cost Value minus your deductible and the calculated depreciation.
  • The Supplement Process: It is very common for the initial estimate to be insufficient. "Hidden damage" under the shingles or decking is often discovered once the tear-off begins. If the contractor finds additional damage, they can work with you to submit a "supplement" to Farmers. This is a formal request for additional funds to cover the full, legitimate cost of the repair. Your contractor should handle the communication and documentation for the supplement, providing photos and explanations for the necessary additional work.

Navigating Common Challenges and Pitfalls

The path to a full and fair settlement isn't always smooth. Be aware of these potential hurdles.

Depreciation and Recoverable Depreciation

If you have an RCV policy, you will not receive the full replacement cost upfront. You receive the ACV first. Once the repairs are completed and you have submitted the final invoice to Farmers, they will release the "recoverable depreciation," which is the withheld amount. Ensure you understand this process to get your full settlement.

When a Claim is Denied or Underpaid

If you disagree with Farmers' assessment or the claim is denied, you have options.

  1. Review the Denial Letter: The insurer is required to provide a specific reason for the denial based on your policy language.
  2. Request a Re-inspection: Ask Farmers to send a different, and perhaps more senior, adjuster for a second look.
  3. Hire a Public Adjuster: A Public Adjuster works for you, not the insurance company. They will re-inspect the damage, handle all the paperwork, and negotiate with Farmers on your behalf. They typically charge a percentage (e.g., 10-20%) of the final settlement.
  4. Seek Legal Counsel: For complex or high-value disputes, consulting with an attorney who specializes in insurance law may be necessary.

Choosing the Right Roofing Contractor

Your choice of contractor can make or break your recovery. Beware of "storm chasers" who descend on damaged neighborhoods with high-pressure sales tactics.

  • Look for Local, Established Companies: Choose a contractor with a physical address in your community and a long track record.
  • Verify Licenses and Insurance: Ensure they are properly licensed and carry both liability insurance and workers' compensation.
  • Check Reviews and References: Read online reviews and ask for a list of local references.
  • Get a Detailed, Written Contract: The contract should include the scope of work, materials to be used (including brand, color, and type of shingles), warranty information, payment schedule, and a projected timeline.
  • Never Sign an Assignment of Benefits (AOB): Be extremely wary of any contractor who asks you to sign an AOB. This document transfers your insurance rights to them, giving them the legal authority to negotiate and collect payment directly from Farmers, which can sometimes lead to disputes and litigation without your control.

Filing a roof insurance claim is a significant undertaking, but with preparation, knowledge, and the right partners, you can navigate it successfully. Your home is your sanctuary. By taking a methodical and informed approach with Farmers Insurance, you can ensure it is restored to a safe, secure, and beautiful state, ready to weather whatever the future may hold.
