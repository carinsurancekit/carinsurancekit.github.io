Travel Insurance for Glamping Trips: Cancellation Tips

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

Glamping—glamorous camping—has become one of the fastest-growing travel trends in recent years. Combining the luxury of high-end accommodations with the raw beauty of nature, it’s no surprise that more travelers are opting for glamping over traditional camping or even conventional hotel stays. However, as the world faces increasing unpredictability—from extreme weather events to geopolitical tensions—securing the right travel insurance for your glamping trip has never been more critical.

Why Glamping Trips Need Specialized Travel Insurance

Unlike standard vacations, glamping often involves remote locations, unique accommodations (like yurts, treehouses, or safari tents), and activities that may not be covered under a basic travel insurance policy. Here’s why you should consider specialized coverage:

1. Unpredictable Weather and Natural Disasters

With climate change intensifying storms, wildfires, and floods, many glamping sites—especially those in wilderness areas—are at higher risk of last-minute cancellations. A robust travel insurance policy can reimburse you for non-refundable deposits if extreme weather forces your trip to be canceled.

2. Remote Locations and Medical Emergencies

Many glamping spots are far from hospitals, making emergency medical evacuation coverage essential. Standard health insurance may not cover remote rescues, so verifying your policy includes this benefit is crucial.

3. Supplier Bankruptcy or Closure

The travel industry has seen significant disruptions in recent years, with some glamping operators shutting down unexpectedly. "Cancel for Any Reason" (CFAR) add-ons can provide flexibility if your chosen site goes out of business before your trip.

Key Features to Look for in Glamping Travel Insurance

Not all travel insurance policies are created equal. Here’s what to prioritize when selecting coverage for your glamping adventure:

Trip Cancellation and Interruption Coverage

This should be the foundation of your policy. Look for plans that cover cancellations due to:
- Illness or injury (yours or a family member’s)
- Severe weather or natural disasters
- Work-related emergencies (e.g., sudden mandatory overtime)

"Cancel for Any Reason" (CFAR) Upgrade

While more expensive, CFAR allows you to cancel your trip for reasons not listed in standard policies—such as sudden concerns about safety or simply changing your mind. Most CFAR policies reimburse 50-75% of your trip costs.

Adventure Activity Coverage

Many glamping experiences include activities like horseback riding, kayaking, or hiking. Standard policies may exclude these, so ensure your plan covers any excursions you’ve booked.

Emergency Medical and Evacuation

Since glamping sites are often off-grid, confirm that your policy includes:
- Coverage for emergency medical treatment
- Air ambulance or evacuation services
- Repatriation of remains (in worst-case scenarios)

How to Minimize Cancellation Risks

Even with insurance, avoiding cancellations is ideal. Here are proactive steps to reduce risks:

Book with Flexible Cancellation Policies

Many glamping operators now offer free cancellations up to a certain date. Always read the fine print before booking.

Monitor Weather and Travel Advisories

Sign up for alerts from sources like the National Weather Service or your government’s travel advisory site. If a storm or political unrest is predicted, consider rescheduling early.

Purchase Insurance Immediately After Booking

Most policies only cover unforeseen events that occur after purchase. Buying early ensures you’re protected if an issue arises months before your trip.

Real-World Scenarios: When Insurance Saved the Trip

Case 1: Wildfire Evacuation

A couple booked a luxury glamping stay in California but had to cancel when wildfires broke out near the site. Their travel insurance reimbursed them for the non-refundable $2,500 booking.

Case 2: Sudden Illness Before Departure

A solo traveler fell ill with a severe infection a week before their glamping retreat in Colorado. Their policy covered the full cost of the trip, including pre-paid guided hikes.

Case 3: Supplier Bankruptcy

A family’s glamping resort in Bali abruptly closed due to financial troubles. Their CFAR add-on allowed them to recover 75% of their expenses.

Final Thoughts on Choosing the Right Provider

Not all insurers understand the nuances of glamping. Companies like World Nomads, Allianz, and Travelex often offer specialized adventure travel policies. Compare quotes and read reviews to find the best fit for your needs.

By investing in the right travel insurance and staying informed, you can enjoy the magic of glamping without the stress of unexpected cancellations. Whether you’re sleeping under the stars in a geodesic dome or unwinding in a safari tent, peace of mind is the ultimate luxury.
