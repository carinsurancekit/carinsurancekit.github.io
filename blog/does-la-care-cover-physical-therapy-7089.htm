Does LA Care Cover Physical Therapy?

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

Physical therapy is a crucial aspect of healthcare, especially in today’s fast-paced world where injuries, chronic pain, and mobility issues are increasingly common. For residents of Los Angeles relying on LA Care Health Plan, understanding whether physical therapy is covered can make a significant difference in managing health and wellness. But beyond just coverage, how does physical therapy fit into the broader healthcare landscape, particularly in light of current global challenges like rising healthcare costs, an aging population, and the aftermath of the COVID-19 pandemic?

Understanding LA Care’s Coverage for Physical Therapy

LA Care, California’s largest publicly operated health plan, provides coverage for a wide range of medical services, including physical therapy (PT). However, the specifics of coverage depend on the type of plan you have—whether it’s Medi-Cal, L.A. Care Covered™, or a Medicare Advantage plan.

What Types of Physical Therapy Are Covered?

Generally, LA Care covers medically necessary physical therapy services, which may include:
- Post-surgical rehabilitation (e.g., after knee or hip replacement)
- Injury recovery (such as sports-related or accident-related injuries)
- Chronic pain management (like back pain or arthritis)
- Neurological rehabilitation (for stroke or Parkinson’s disease patients)

However, coverage may require prior authorization, meaning your doctor must confirm that PT is necessary before sessions begin.

Are There Limits on Sessions?

Some plans may impose visit limits (e.g., 20-30 sessions per year), while others may allow more based on medical necessity. It’s essential to check your specific plan details or contact LA Care directly.

The Bigger Picture: Why Physical Therapy Matters in 2024

Physical therapy isn’t just about recovery—it’s about prevention, long-term wellness, and reducing dependency on pain medications. In a world grappling with opioid addiction and rising healthcare costs, PT offers a non-invasive, cost-effective alternative.

The Opioid Crisis and PT as an Alternative

The U.S. has been fighting an opioid epidemic for years, with millions addicted to prescription painkillers. Physical therapy can help reduce reliance on opioids by addressing pain through movement, strength training, and manual therapy. LA Care’s inclusion of PT in coverage aligns with broader efforts to promote safer pain management.

Aging Population and Increased Need for Mobility Care

With baby boomers aging, demand for physical therapy is skyrocketing. Conditions like arthritis, osteoporosis, and stroke recovery require ongoing PT to maintain independence. LA Care’s coverage helps seniors access these vital services without financial strain.

Post-Pandemic Rehabilitation Challenges

Long COVID has left many with persistent fatigue, muscle weakness, and mobility issues. Physical therapy plays a key role in helping patients regain strength and function. LA Care’s PT benefits are especially relevant now as more people seek rehabilitation after severe COVID cases.

How to Access Physical Therapy Through LA Care

If you’re an LA Care member and need PT, follow these steps:

1. Check Your Plan Details

Log into your LA Care account or call customer service to confirm your PT benefits.

2. Get a Referral or Prior Authorization

Most plans require a doctor’s referral before starting PT. Your provider will submit necessary paperwork to LA Care.

3. Find an In-Network Provider

LA Care has a network of approved physical therapists. Going out-of-network may result in higher costs or denial of coverage.

4. Understand Your Costs

Depending on your plan, you may have copays or coinsurance for each PT session. Some Medi-Cal plans cover PT at no extra cost.

Common Misconceptions About Physical Therapy Coverage

“I Don’t Need a Doctor’s Referral”

While some insurance plans allow direct access to PT, LA Care typically requires a referral for coverage. Skipping this step could mean paying out-of-pocket.

“All PT Clinics Accept LA Care”

Not every clinic is in-network. Always verify before scheduling appointments to avoid unexpected bills.

“PT Is Only for Serious Injuries”

Physical therapy isn’t just for post-surgery recovery—it can help with posture correction, balance issues, and even preventive care.

Final Thoughts

Navigating healthcare coverage can be complex, but understanding your benefits ensures you get the care you need. With LA Care covering physical therapy in many cases, members have a valuable resource for maintaining mobility, managing pain, and improving overall quality of life—especially in a world where health challenges are constantly evolving.

Whether you're recovering from an injury, managing a chronic condition, or dealing with long-term COVID effects, physical therapy could be the key to a healthier, more active future. Always consult your provider and LA Care to maximize your benefits effectively.
