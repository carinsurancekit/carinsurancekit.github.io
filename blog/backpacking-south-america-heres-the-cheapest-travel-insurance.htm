Backpacking South America? Here’s the Cheapest Travel Insurance

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

South America has always been a magnet for adventurers—its sprawling Amazon rainforests, Andean peaks, and vibrant cultures call out to those with a backpack and a thirst for discovery. But let’s be real: the world isn’t the same as it was a decade ago. Between climate-related disruptions, political instability, and the lingering echoes of a global pandemic, traveling today requires more than just a sturdy pair of boots and a rough itinerary. It demands preparedness. And at the heart of preparedness? Solid, affordable travel insurance.

You might think skipping insurance is a good way to save money. After all, when you’re budgeting for hostels, street food, and bus rides across the continent, every dollar counts. But in a region where altitude sickness, unexpected strikes (paros), or even a stolen phone can derail your trip, going without coverage is a high-stakes gamble. The good news? You don’t have to break the bank to stay protected.

Why Travel Insurance is Non-Negotiable in 2024

We’re navigating a travel landscape shaped by global upheavals. Here’s why insurance isn’t optional anymore:

Political Unrest & Sudden Strikes

Countries like Peru, Bolivia, and Chile have experienced social protests and transportation halts with little warning. A roadblock (bloqueo) might leave you stranded for days, leading to extra accommodation costs and missed flights. Good insurance can cover these unexpected expenses.

Climate Chaos & Natural Disasters

From flooding in Brazil to earthquakes in Ecuador, climate volatility is real. Hiking in Patagonia? Weather shifts are extreme and unpredictable. Emergency evacuation—which can cost tens of thousands of dollars—is one of the most critical features your policy should include.

Health Concerns Beyond Covid-19

While pandemic-related coverage is now standard in many policies, other health risks remain. Dengue fever, traveler’s diarrhea, and altitude sickness (soroche) are common across the continent. Healthcare in private facilities can be excellent but expensive for foreigners.

Theft and Loss

Let’s be honest: petty theft happens. Cell phones, cameras, and passports are common targets in crowded markets or long bus rides. A good policy will cover stolen items and help you navigate the process of replacing documents.

What Does “Cheapest” Really Mean?

Don’t just choose the lowest premium. The cheapest insurance is the one that offers the best value—adequate coverage at a reasonable price. A $30 policy might seem like a steal until you realize it has a $5,000 deductible or doesn’t cover adventure activities like trekking or surfing.

Look for: - Medical coverage of at least $100,000 - Emergency evacuation - Theft and loss protection - Coverage for activities you plan to do (e.g., hiking above a certain altitude, cycling, or volunteering) - 24/7 assistance hotline

Top Budget-Friendly Travel Insurance Options for South America

Based on recent reviews and traveler testimonials, here are some affordable yet reliable options:

SafetyWing

Popular with digital nomads and long-term backpackers, SafetyWing offers flexible, subscription-style insurance. It covers medical expenses, travel delays, and lost luggage, and even includes some Covid-19 coverage. Prices typically start around $40-50 per month.

World Nomads

A longtime favorite among backpackers. While slightly pricier, World Nomads covers a wide range of adventure activities and offers reliable customer support. Their comprehensive plan includes theft and gear protection—ideal if you’re traveling with expensive equipment.

HeyMondo

Known for competitive pricing and a user-friendly app. Heymondo offers single-trip and annual policies with good medical and cancellation coverage. They also include a free medical hotline for consultations.

InsureMyTrip

This is a comparison site that allows you to input your trip details and filter policies based on budget and needs. It’s excellent for finding deals from smaller providers.

How to Keep Your Insurance Costs Down

Choose a Higher Deductible

Opting for a higher deductible (excess) can significantly lower your premium. Just make sure you can afford the out-of-pocket cost if you need to make a claim.

Don’t Over-Insure

If you’re not carrying expensive gear or don’t plan on risky activities, skip add-ons you won’t use.

Buy Longer-Term Coverage

If you’ traveling for several months, look into long-term backpacker insurance rather than several single-trip policies.

Check Your Existing Coverage

Some credit cards (especially premium ones) offer limited travel insurance. Verify what’s included—but don’t assume it’s enough for a months-long trip through multiple countries.

Real Travelers, Real Stories

Maria, a solo traveler from Germany, shared: “I got severe food poisoning in La Paz and ended up in a private clinic. Without insurance, I would’ve paid over $1,200. My Heymondo policy covered everything—even the phone consultation I had before going to the hospital.”

Jake and Tom, backpacking through Colombia: “Our bus was canceled due to a national strike. We missed our flight from Bogotá to Lima, but thanks to World Nomads’ trip interruption coverage, we were reimbursed for the new tickets we had to book.”

Final Tips Before You Buy

  • Read the fine print, especially regarding altitude limits and “high-risk” areas.
  • Keep a digital copy of your policy and the emergency number accessible.
  • Document everything in case of theft or loss—take photos of your belongings and keep receipts.

Travel insurance might not be the most exciting part of your South American adventure, but it’s the safety net that lets you explore with confidence. In today’s unpredictable world, that peace of mind is priceless—even when you’re on a budget.
