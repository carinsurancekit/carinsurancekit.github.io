How to Get Car Insurance After Multiple Claims

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

Navigating the car insurance market after filing multiple claims can feel like an uphill battle. Insurance companies view drivers with a history of claims as high-risk, which often leads to higher premiums or even policy denials. However, with the right strategies, you can still secure affordable coverage. This guide explores actionable steps to help you find car insurance after multiple claims while addressing modern challenges like inflation, climate-related accidents, and evolving insurance technologies.

Understanding Why Multiple Claims Make Insurance Harder to Get

Insurance providers assess risk based on your driving history. The more claims you’ve filed, the more likely they assume you’ll file another. Here’s why:

Increased Risk Perception

Insurers rely on statistical models to predict future claims. Multiple past claims—especially at-fault accidents—signal a higher likelihood of future payouts.

Rising Insurance Costs Globally

Due to inflation, supply chain disruptions, and climate-related disasters (e.g., hurricanes, wildfires), insurers are tightening underwriting standards. Drivers with multiple claims face steeper premium hikes.

Impact of Fraud Prevention Measures

With insurance fraud on the rise, companies scrutinize frequent claimants more carefully. Even legitimate claims may raise red flags.

Steps to Secure Car Insurance After Multiple Claims

1. Shop Around for High-Risk Insurers

Not all insurers treat multiple claims the same. Some specialize in high-risk drivers and may offer better rates. Consider:
- Non-standard insurance companies (e.g., The General, Dairyland)
- Regional insurers (may have more flexible underwriting)
- Independent insurance agents (can compare multiple providers)

2. Improve Your Driving Record

While you can’t erase past claims, you can demonstrate safer habits moving forward:
- Avoid new claims by driving defensively.
- Take a defensive driving course to qualify for discounts.
- Maintain a clean record for at least 3 years—insurers often overlook older claims.

3. Adjust Your Coverage Strategically

If full coverage is too expensive, consider:
- Higher deductibles (lowers premiums but increases out-of-pocket costs).
- Dropping collision coverage (if your car’s value is low).
- Usage-based insurance (pay-as-you-drive programs like Progressive’s Snapshot).

4. Leverage Telematics and AI-Driven Policies

Many insurers now offer usage-based insurance (UBI) programs that monitor driving behavior via apps or devices. Safe driving can offset past claims:
- Progressive Snapshot
- State Farm Drive Safe & Save
- Allstate Drivewise

5. Bundle Policies for Discounts

Combining auto insurance with homeowners or renters insurance can reduce overall costs. Companies like State Farm and Geico offer multi-policy discounts.

6. Consider a Named-Driver Exclusion (As a Last Resort)

If one driver on your policy has a poor record, excluding them may lower premiums—but they won’t be covered when driving.

How Climate Change and Inflation Affect Your Options

Climate-Related Claims Surge

Natural disasters (floods, hailstorms) have led to a spike in comprehensive claims. Insurers in high-risk areas (e.g., Florida, California) are raising rates or pulling out entirely. If your claims were weather-related:
- Look for insurers still operating in your region.
- Consider catastrophe-specific coverage if available.

Inflation’s Impact on Repair Costs

Rising labor and parts costs mean insurers pay more for claims. This trickles down to higher premiums for all drivers, especially those with multiple claims.

The Future of Insurance for High-Risk Drivers

AI and Dynamic Pricing

Insurers are using AI to personalize rates based on real-time data. Safe drivers may see lower premiums despite past claims.

Peer-to-Peer Insurance Models

Startups like Turo (for rentals) and P2P auto insurance platforms could offer alternatives for high-risk drivers.

Government-Backed Insurance Pools

In some states, high-risk drivers can access coverage through assigned risk pools, though premiums are often expensive.

Final Tips for Long-Term Savings

  • Build credit—many insurers use credit scores to set rates.
  • Drive less—low-mileage discounts can help.
  • Stay claim-free—time is your best ally in reducing premium costs.

By understanding the challenges and exploring all available options, you can still find affordable car insurance—even after multiple claims.
